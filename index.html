The travel story of the week
Read the top story in the travel industry this week!

In 2008, a man named Jason Lewis completed a 13-year journey that took him around the world using only human power. He began his journey in London, England, and traveled eastward, using a combination of rollerblading, cycling, inline skating, kayaking, rowing, and walking to cover over 46,000 miles across Europe, Asia, North America, South America, and Africa. He became the first person to circumnavigate the globe using only human power, and his journey set several world records. Along the way, he faced many challenges, including injuries, equipment failures, and hostile political situations, but he persevered and finally returned to London on November 18, 2008.

Moremi: The Yoruba Woman who Sacrificed Everything
Remember the young queen who gave up her only son for Yoruba land?

Moremi was a princess in ancient Nigeria who is remembered for her bravery and selflessness. When her kingdom was invaded, she offered herself as a sacrifice to the gods in order to save her people. The gods accepted her offer and granted her the power to defeat the invading army. Moremi disguised herself as a slave and entered the enemy camp, gathering information that helped her people win the war. After the war, Moremi returned home as a hero and her story is now an important part of Yoruba folklore.
